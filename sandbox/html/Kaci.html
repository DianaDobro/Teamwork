Katy Perry

Basic information

Katy Perry, real name Katheryn Elizabeth Hudson, was born on 25 October 1984 in Santa Barbara, California. She chose her mother's maiden name, Perry, as her stage name. She has got two siblings. Younger brother David Hudson and older sister Angela Hudson.

Her both parents were pastors, so they had to move frequently due to their parents' reassignments to different churches around the country. By the time Katy was 11, they had settled back in Santa Barbara. During this time, her parents had occasional financial problems.

Young Katy listened primarily to gospel music, as she had no chance at all to get into any other genre in her religious family. Later, through her friends, she discovered the magic of pop music. She started recording cassettes and singing songs to her parents who saw talent in her. From the age of 9 she took singing lessons and by the age of 17 she was singing in the church where her parents worked. On her 13th birthday, she got a guitar and started performing publicly with songs she wrote herself. Outside that, she enjoyed rollerblading, skateboarding, surfing the waves of the Pacific Ocean and took dance lessons.


Her unusual hobby

Katy Perry likes to play with her hair, but you probably didn't know that the singer is into collecting other celebrities' hair. She even spoke openly about it at a fan meet and greet, admitting that she keeps Miley Cyrus and Taylor Swift locks in her purse. "It's totally creepy but amazing," she said.



The beginnings of a promising career

Katy Perry was literally passionate about music, as evidenced by the fact that she completed her GED (General Educational Development) requirements as a freshman in high school and left Santa Barbara's Dos Pueblos High School at 15 to pursue music full-time. She also briefly studied Italian opera at the Music Academy of the West in Santa Barbara. Katy's talent was noticed by artists Steve Thomas and Jennifer Knapp of Nashville, Tennessee. They took Katy under their wing and began to further develop her talent. In Nashville, she began recording her first demos and playing guitar. She released her first studio album, Katy Hudson, on March 6, 2001 under the auspices of Red Hill Records. The album was in the gospel style and was positively received by critics, but was commercially unsuccessful with 200 units sold. After this album, she quit gospel music and began working with producer Glen Ballard.

She moved to Los Angeles at the age of 17 and briefly performed as Katheryn Perry in 2003 to avoid confusion with famous actress Kate Hudson. She later began using the stage name Katy Perry.

Perry's song "Simple" appeared on the soundtrack to the 2005 film The Sisterhood of the Traveling Pants (directed by Ken Kwapis). She also collaborated on the song "Old Habits Die Hard" by Mick Jagger, frontman of the legendary Rolling Stones, which was featured on the soundtrack of the romantic comedy Alfie (dir. Charles Shyer, 2004). The song won the award for Best Original Song at the 62nd Golden Globe Awards.

In 2008, Katy Perry participated in the Warped tour, a rock festival touring the US and Canada, held every summer since 1995. In November 2008, she was given the role of presenter at the MTV Europe Music Awards, from which Perry herself took home the Best New Act award. She then won the International Female Solo Artist award at the Brit Awards in February 2009, beating out such stars as Beyonc√© and Pink.

During the tour she toured almost the entire world, performing in North America, Asia, Oceania and Europe.
